Fertility and In Vitro Fertilization (IVF)

Pregnancy signifies a joyous and fulfilling phase in a woman's life, the reality is that not every woman can achieve biological conception naturally.  The journey toward conception can be laden with stress and emotional turmoil. Approximately 10% of couples require assistance to conceive, prompting gynecologists to refer them to IVF specialists. In Vitro Fertilization (IVF), an assisted reproductive technology (ART), emerges as a beacon of hope in such situations.  IVF involves the intricate process of extracting eggs, obtaining a sperm sample, and manually combining the two outside the mother's womb. Following fertilization, the resulting embryos undergo incubation before being carefully transferred back into the woman's uterus, holding the promise of successful implantation and pregnancy.

Who can benifit from IVE?

IVF may be recommended for individuals or couples facing various fertility challenges, including:

  • Blocked or damaged fallopian tubes
  • Unexplained infertility
  • Male factor infertility, such as low sperm count or motility
  • Ovulation disorders
  • Endometriosis
  • Genetic disorders
  • Age-related infertility
